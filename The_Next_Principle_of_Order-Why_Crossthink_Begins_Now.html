Introduction

In 2025, the geopolitical incident surrounding the Harvard University blockade exposed not merely a conflict between a government and a university, but a deepening rift within the global order itself. The event signaled the regression of international society into an era where raw power once again trumps intellectual discourse and shared values. 1. The Collapse of "Universal Values" and the Rise of Civilizational Spheres

For decades, values like freedom, democracy, and human rights were regarded as universal. However, the 21st century has revealed their fragile foundations. Especially within the Global South, alternative visions and counter-values have risen, challenging the West-centric paradigm. What we are witnessing is not merely the decline of liberal internationalism, but the birth of a world fragmented into civilizational blocs, each bearing its own epistemologies, ethics, and historical narratives.

This shift is not ideological but civilizational: plural, affective, and deeply rooted in lived identities. In such a world, the Crossthink project begins with the recognition that no single value system can claim absolute legitimacy. Instead, it calls for structures that embrace plurality without collapsing into relativism.
